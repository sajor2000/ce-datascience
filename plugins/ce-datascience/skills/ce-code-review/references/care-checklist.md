# CARE Checklist for Clinical Case Reports

CAse REport Guidelines (CARE).
13-item checklist applicable to clinical case reports.

**Primary reference:** Gagnier JJ, Kienle G, Altman DG, Moher D, Sox H, Riley D; CARE Group. The CARE Guidelines: Consensus-based Clinical Case Reporting Guideline Development. Glob Adv Health Med. 2013;2(5):38-43. doi:10.7453/gahmj.2013.008. PMID: 24416692.

Each item includes the checklist number, section, description, and what to look for
in analysis code and outputs when evaluating compliance.

---

## Title

### Item 1 -- Title

**Description:** The words "case report" should appear in the title along with the area of focus most interesting to readers.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Report title or rendered document header containing "case report" and the primary diagnosis or clinical finding
- Title template that identifies the document as a case report
- Manuscript or report output whose title signals the case report format and clinical focus area

---

## Key Words

### Item 2 -- Key words

**Description:** Two to five key words that identify diagnoses or interventions in this case report, including "case report" as a key word.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Keyword metadata in report template or document header including "case report"
- Two to five clinically relevant keywords (diagnosis, intervention, complication) listed in output
- Keywords matching MeSH terms or standardized vocabulary for indexing
- Document metadata fields populated with appropriate keywords

---

## Abstract

### Item 3 -- Abstract

**Description:** Introduction -- what does this case add? The main symptoms and/or important clinical findings of the patient, the main diagnoses, therapeutic interventions, and outcomes. Conclusion -- the main "take-away" lessons from this case report.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Structured abstract with introduction, case summary, and conclusion sections
- Abstract output that describes what the case adds to existing literature
- Key clinical findings, diagnoses, interventions, and outcomes summarized concisely
- Take-away lessons or clinical implications stated in the abstract conclusion

---

## Introduction

### Item 4 -- Introduction

**Description:** Brief background summary of this case referencing the relevant medical literature.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Introductory section citing relevant medical literature establishing context for the case
- Background information on the condition, its prevalence, and why this case is noteworthy
- References to prior case reports or series addressing similar presentations
- Statement of what this case contributes to the existing body of knowledge

---

## Patient Information

### Item 5 -- Patient information

**Description:** De-identified demographic and other patient information. Main concerns and symptoms of the patient. Medical, family, and psychosocial history including diet, lifestyle, and genetic information. Relevant past interventions with outcomes.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- De-identified demographic data presented (age, sex, ethnicity without identifiers)
- Chief complaint and presenting symptoms documented
- Medical history, family history, and psychosocial history summarized
- Prior treatments and their outcomes described
- Confirmation that all patient identifiers have been removed or anonymized

---

## Clinical Findings

### Item 6 -- Clinical findings

**Description:** Describe the relevant physical examination (PE) findings.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Physical examination findings documented with pertinent positives and negatives
- Vital signs and relevant clinical measurements reported
- Focused examination findings related to the presenting complaint
- Objective clinical data (lab values, imaging findings) presented alongside PE findings

---

## Timeline

### Item 7 -- Timeline

**Description:** Depict important dates and times in this case in a timeline (figure or table).

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Timeline figure or table generated showing key dates (symptom onset, presentation, diagnosis, treatment, follow-up)
- Chronological ordering of clinical events with dates or time intervals
- Visual timeline rendering code or structured timeline data output
- Key milestones clearly delineated (initial presentation, diagnostic workup, intervention, outcome assessment)

---

## Diagnostic Assessment

### Item 8 -- Diagnostic assessment

**Description:** Diagnostic methods (such as PE, laboratory testing, imaging, surveys, questionnaires). Diagnostic challenges (such as access to testing, financial, cultural). Diagnostic reasoning including differential diagnosis. Prognostic characteristics (such as staging) where applicable.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Diagnostic workup documented with tests ordered and results
- Laboratory values, imaging reports, and pathology findings presented with reference ranges
- Differential diagnosis list and reasoning for final diagnosis
- Diagnostic challenges or barriers documented
- Staging or prognostic classification where applicable

---

## Therapeutic Intervention

### Item 9 -- Therapeutic intervention

**Description:** Types of intervention (such as pharmacologic, surgical, preventive, self-care). Administration of intervention (such as dosage, strength, duration). Changes in intervention (with rationale).

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Interventions described with specific details (drug name, dose, route, frequency, duration)
- Surgical or procedural details documented
- Treatment modifications and rationale for changes
- Supportive care measures and patient education documented
- Timeline of therapeutic interventions relative to clinical course

---

## Follow-up and Outcomes

### Item 10 -- Follow-up and outcomes

**Description:** Clinician-assessed and patient-assessed outcomes when appropriate. Important follow-up diagnostic and other test results. Intervention adherence and tolerability. Adverse and unanticipated events.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Follow-up visit data with clinician assessments and patient-reported outcomes
- Follow-up diagnostic test results (lab values, imaging) with comparison to baseline
- Treatment adherence and tolerability documented
- Adverse events or complications recorded
- Final outcome status clearly stated (resolved, improved, stable, worsened, deceased)

---

## Discussion

### Item 11 -- Discussion

**Description:** Strengths and limitations of the management of this case. Relevant medical literature. Rationale for conclusions (including assessments of cause and effect). The primary "take-away" lessons of this case report.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Discussion section analyzing strengths and limitations of the case management
- Comparison with published literature and similar cases
- Causal reasoning discussed with appropriate caveats for single-case inference
- Key lessons and clinical implications clearly articulated
- Suggestions for future research or clinical practice changes based on this case

---

## Patient Perspective

### Item 12 -- Patient perspective

**Description:** The patient should share his or her perspective or experience whenever possible.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Patient perspective section included in the report when obtainable
- Patient-reported experience or satisfaction with care documented
- Qualitative patient input (direct quotes or summarized perspective)
- Documentation of why patient perspective was not included if absent

---

## Informed Consent

### Item 13 -- Informed consent

**Description:** The patient should provide informed consent for the publication of their case.

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Informed consent statement in the report confirming patient authorization for publication
- Consent documentation referenced (consent form, IRB approval, waiver)
- Statement of de-identification measures taken to protect patient privacy
- IRB or ethics committee approval referenced when applicable

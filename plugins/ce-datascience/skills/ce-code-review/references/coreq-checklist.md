# COREQ Checklist for Qualitative Research

Consolidated Criteria for Reporting Qualitative Research (COREQ).
32-item checklist applicable to qualitative research (interviews and focus groups).

**Primary reference:** Tong A, Sainsbury P, Craig J. Consolidated criteria for reporting qualitative research (COREQ): a 32-item checklist for interviews and focus groups. Int J Qual Health Care. 2007;19(6):349-357. doi:10.1093/intqhc/mzm042. PMID: 17872937.

Each item includes the checklist number, section, description, and what to look for
in analysis code and outputs when evaluating compliance.

---

## Domain 1: Research Team and Reflexivity -- Personal Characteristics

### Item 1 -- Interviewer/facilitator

**Description:** Which author(s) conducted the interview or focus group?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Documentation identifying which team member(s) conducted data collection
- Interviewer or facilitator names listed in methods section or protocol
- Codebook or analysis log attributing transcripts to specific interviewers
- Team role assignments documented in project files

### Item 2 -- Credentials

**Description:** What were the researcher's credentials (e.g., PhD, MD)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Researcher credentials listed in author information or methods documentation
- Professional qualifications relevant to the research topic documented
- Team roster with academic or clinical credentials in project files

### Item 3 -- Occupation

**Description:** What was their occupation at the time of the study?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Researcher occupations and institutional affiliations documented
- Professional roles relevant to the study topic described
- Departmental or organizational affiliation in project metadata

### Item 4 -- Gender

**Description:** Was the researcher male or female?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Researcher gender documented in methods or reflexivity section
- Gender composition of the research team reported
- Potential gender dynamics in data collection acknowledged

### Item 5 -- Experience and training

**Description:** What experience or training did the researcher have?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Qualitative research training or experience documented for each team member
- Prior experience with the study population or topic described
- Methodological training (interview techniques, focus group facilitation) noted
- Number of prior qualitative studies conducted referenced

## Domain 1: Research Team and Reflexivity -- Relationship with Participants

### Item 6 -- Relationship established

**Description:** Was a relationship established prior to study commencement?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Documentation of any prior relationship between researchers and participants
- Pre-existing clinical, professional, or social relationships disclosed
- Steps taken to establish rapport before data collection described

### Item 7 -- Participant knowledge of the interviewer

**Description:** What did the participants know about the researcher (e.g., personal goals, reasons for doing the research)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Information shared with participants about the researcher documented
- Participant information sheet or consent form content describing the researcher's role
- Documentation of what participants were told about the study purpose and researcher motivations

### Item 8 -- Interviewer characteristics

**Description:** What characteristics were reported about the interviewer/facilitator (e.g., bias, assumptions, reasons and interests in the research topic)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Reflexivity statement in methods or a dedicated reflexivity section
- Researcher assumptions, biases, or preconceptions about the topic documented
- Personal motivations or interests in the research topic disclosed
- Bracketing or reflexive journaling described as part of the methodology

---

## Domain 2: Study Design -- Theoretical Framework

### Item 9 -- Methodological orientation and theory

**Description:** What methodological orientation was stated to underpin the study (e.g., grounded theory, discourse analysis, ethnography, phenomenology, content analysis)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Qualitative methodology explicitly named (grounded theory, phenomenology, thematic analysis, etc.)
- Theoretical framework or philosophical underpinning described
- Methodological references cited to support the chosen approach
- Analytical approach aligned with the stated theoretical orientation

---

## Domain 2: Study Design -- Participant Selection

### Item 10 -- Sampling

**Description:** How were participants selected (e.g., purposive, convenience, consecutive, snowball)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Sampling strategy explicitly named and justified
- Purposive sampling criteria documented (maximum variation, typical case, critical case)
- Recruitment code or scripts that implement the sampling strategy
- Participant selection criteria and recruitment pathway described

### Item 11 -- Method of approach

**Description:** How were participants approached (e.g., face-to-face, telephone, mail, email)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Recruitment approach documented (in-person, telephone, email, posted letter, referral)
- Recruitment materials (letters, emails, flyers) included in project files
- Initial contact method and subsequent follow-up procedures described

### Item 12 -- Sample size

**Description:** How many participants were in the study?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Total participant count clearly reported
- Sample size per group if multiple groups were included
- Justification for sample size (saturation, information power, pragmatic constraints)
- Participant demographics table generated in outputs

### Item 13 -- Non-participation

**Description:** How many people refused to participate or dropped out? Reasons?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Number of individuals who declined participation documented
- Dropout counts and reasons recorded
- Response rate or participation rate calculated
- Comparison of participants and non-participants when feasible

## Domain 2: Study Design -- Setting

### Item 14 -- Setting of data collection

**Description:** Where was the data collected (e.g., home, clinic, workplace)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Data collection setting documented (clinic, home, community venue, virtual/online)
- Physical environment description relevant to participant comfort and disclosure
- Multiple sites documented if applicable

### Item 15 -- Presence of non-participants

**Description:** Was anyone else present besides the participants and researchers?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Documentation of who was present during data collection sessions
- Presence of interpreters, caregivers, or observers noted
- Potential influence of non-participants on data quality discussed

### Item 16 -- Description of sample

**Description:** What are the important characteristics of the sample (e.g., demographic data, date)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Participant characteristics table with relevant demographics
- Clinical or experiential characteristics relevant to the research question summarized
- Sample diversity documented relative to the population of interest
- Date range of data collection reported

---

## Domain 2: Study Design -- Data Collection

### Item 17 -- Interview guide

**Description:** Were questions, prompts, guides provided by the authors? Was it pilot tested?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Interview guide or topic guide included in project files or appendices
- Semi-structured interview questions documented
- Pilot testing of the guide described with any resulting modifications
- Prompts and probes listed alongside main questions

### Item 18 -- Repeat interviews

**Description:** Were repeat interviews carried out? If yes, how many?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Documentation of whether participants were interviewed more than once
- Number of repeat interviews and rationale (member checking, longitudinal design)
- Participant tracking showing single vs. multiple data collection points

### Item 19 -- Audio/visual recording

**Description:** Did the research use audio or visual recording to collect the data?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Recording method documented (audio, video, field notes only)
- Equipment specifications or recording software noted
- Consent for recording documented in consent procedures
- Recording quality and any technical issues noted

### Item 20 -- Field notes

**Description:** Were field notes made during and/or after the interview or focus group?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Field note documentation strategy described
- Field notes included in the analysis dataset or referenced in coding
- Timing of field note creation (during, immediately after, end of day) documented
- Content of field notes described (observations, reflections, contextual details)

### Item 21 -- Duration

**Description:** What was the duration of the interviews or focus groups?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Interview or focus group duration reported (range, mean, median)
- Duration data tracked per session in project logs
- Total data collection time span (dates from first to last session) documented

### Item 22 -- Data saturation

**Description:** Was data saturation discussed?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Data saturation explicitly discussed and defined
- Point at which saturation was reached documented (after N interviews, no new themes)
- Saturation assessment method described (thematic saturation, code saturation, meaning saturation)
- Documentation that recruitment continued until saturation or justification if not

### Item 23 -- Transcripts returned

**Description:** Were transcripts returned to participants for comment and/or correction?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Member checking or transcript review process documented
- Participant feedback on transcripts incorporated into analysis
- Documentation of whether participants corrected or amended their transcripts
- Reasons for not returning transcripts documented if not performed

## Domain 3: Analysis and Findings -- Data Analysis

### Item 24 -- Number of data coders

**Description:** How many data coders coded the data?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Number of coders documented in methods
- Coding team composition and roles described
- Single vs. multiple coder approach justified

### Item 25 -- Description of the coding tree

**Description:** Did authors provide a description of the coding tree?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Codebook or coding tree included in project files or appendices
- Hierarchical code structure documented (parent codes, child codes)
- Code definitions and inclusion/exclusion criteria for each code provided
- Evolution of the coding framework described (initial to final)

### Item 26 -- Derivation of themes

**Description:** Were themes identified in advance or derived from the data?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Inductive vs. deductive approach to theme derivation documented
- Process of moving from codes to categories to themes described
- A priori framework specified if deductive approach used
- Theme development audit trail available

### Item 27 -- Software

**Description:** What software, if applicable, was used to manage the data?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Qualitative data analysis software named (NVivo, ATLAS.ti, MAXQDA, Dedoose, R/RQDA)
- Software version documented
- Manual coding methods described if no software used
- Data management procedures for organizing transcripts and codes

### Item 28 -- Participant checking

**Description:** Did participants provide feedback on the findings?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Member checking of findings documented (presenting themes to participants for validation)
- Participant feedback on interpreted themes incorporated
- Method of member checking described (individual, group, written summary)
- Impact of participant feedback on final themes documented

---

## Domain 3: Analysis and Findings -- Reporting

### Item 29 -- Quotations presented

**Description:** Were participant quotations presented to illustrate the themes/findings? Was each quotation identified (e.g., participant number)?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Direct quotations included in report output with participant identifiers (pseudonyms, ID numbers)
- Quotations linked to specific themes or categories
- Range of participants represented in quotations (not over-reliance on a few voices)
- Quotation selection rationale documented

### Item 30 -- Data and findings consistent

**Description:** Was there consistency between the data presented and the findings?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Themes supported by multiple quotations or data excerpts
- Negative or deviant cases presented alongside dominant themes
- Findings logically derived from the presented data
- Audit trail connecting raw data to interpreted themes

### Item 31 -- Clarity of major themes

**Description:** Were major themes clearly presented in the findings?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Major themes clearly named, defined, and presented with supporting data
- Thematic structure organized logically (thematic map, hierarchy, or narrative)
- Subthemes nested within major themes where appropriate
- Theme prevalence or distribution across participants documented

### Item 32 -- Clarity of minor themes

**Description:** Is there a description of diverse cases or discussion of minor themes?

**What to look for in code/outputs:**
- Minor themes or outlier perspectives documented alongside major findings
- Deviant case analysis included
- Diversity of perspectives within the sample acknowledged
- Discussion of themes that did not fit the dominant pattern
